# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want Phyx to be a welcoming, respectful, and productive project for everyone. Contributors, maintainers, and users are expected to participate with empathy and professionalism.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- using welcoming and inclusive language;
- respecting different viewpoints and experience levels;
- giving and accepting constructive feedback gracefully;
- focusing criticism on ideas and code, not people;
- being mindful that open-source work often happens in limited personal time.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, insults, threats, or personal attacks;
- discriminatory language or behavior;
- trolling, sustained disruption, or bad-faith arguments;
- publishing private information without explicit permission;
- sexualized language or unwelcome attention;
- any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject contributions, or block participants when necessary to protect the project and its community.

Enforcement should be proportionate and focused on restoring a healthy collaboration environment.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the maintainer privately through GitHub. Please include relevant context, links, screenshots, or examples when possible.

Reports will be handled with care and confidentiality where practical.

## Attribution

This code of conduct is inspired by the Contributor Covenant and adapted for the current size and scope of Phyx.
